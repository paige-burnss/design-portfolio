Sustainability


When I was still a youtube obsessed middle schooler, I came across my first exposure to zero waste living. Lauren Singer is a youtuber and small business owner, and she keeps all the trash she makes throughout multiple years in a single mason jar.

During high school I was too busy to explore sustainability, but in college I’ve taken the opportunity to learn more about it and how I can change my habits to be more sustainable.

ENLP 3100: COMPLEX LEADERSHIP CHALLENGES

In this class, I completed a semester-long research project on Zero Waste Living. I spent two months learning as much as I could about this trend by looking through different perspectives, then I compiled all of my research in a blog and final project.

I spent a week living with zero waste and video taped it all on my phone so that I could share my experience. I took this class in Fall of 2021, but I’m still doing most of the zero waste practices I started doing in that video!

SHOWA WOMEN’S UNIVERSITY JOINT PROJECT

As a freshman, I was given the opportunity to work with students who attended the Showa Women’s University in Japan on a case competition style project. There were several topics that we could choose to work with, and I chose Climate Policy.

Our project was to research current climate policies in both Japan and the US and come up with a joint solution so each country could improve the way they are slowing climate change. We proposed 3 solutions. The first was specific to the US, to create a climate caucus in Congress. The second was specific to Japan, to use ammonia fuel instead of fossil fuels for transportation. And the final was for both countries to start using agrivoltaics, a specific design of solar panels, and to sign an energy and climate agreement.

PRLC 1820: FOUNDATIONS IN LEADERSHIP 2

In this class, my recitation and I had to teach a full class on a problem in the world. Broad topic right? My group took this prompt and decided to focus on food waste and how that affects the environment.

We incorporated examples of solutions to these problems that are present in our local community in Boulder and Colorado, as well as individual changes everyone could make to reduce their waste.
