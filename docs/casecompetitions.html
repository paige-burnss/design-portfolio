Case Competitions


I had my first experience with case competitions after my freshman year of high school at Junior Achievement Business week, where I was tasked to design a new Otterbox case attachment, and accompany that design with a marketing plan and financial analysis.

That experience exposed me to the fast paced, solution oriented environment of business, and I was hooked! In high school, I also competed in the CU Women in Business Case Competition after my junior year, designing a marketing initiative for female menstrual products.

Case competitions have helped me develop the following skills:
  • Financial Analysis
  • Problem Solving
  • Developing Creative Solutions
  • Designing Visually Pleasing and Effective Slideshows
  • Public Speaking
Since I’ve started school at the Leeds School of Business, I’ve taken every opportunity to strengthen these skills in case competitions.


CU BUSINESS ETHICS CASE COMPETITION 2022

Third Place
This case was based on the current events surrounding the COVID vaccine, and whether or not a multi-national company should require their employees to get the vaccine or not.

My team looked at this problem in depth, not just from the point of view of whether COVID vaccines would make the company more productive. We decided to require vaccines, with the exception of medical and religious reasons to not get the vaccine. We also took into account that some countries the company operated in wouldn’t have as much access to the vaccines, and developed a plan to make the vaccines accessible.

Previous Next

The second part of the case brought up an issue of many executives prepared to leave because of the vaccine requirement, several of which were key to the success of the business.

Our team stuck to our “no exceptions other than medical and religious” decision, and came up with a strategy to retain most if not all of the executives using a workplace empowerment plan and Harvard Business Review retention framework.

CU DANIELS ETHICS CASE COMPETITION 2021

First Place Winner
This case was focused on a company that worked with lots of classified information. They had strict privacy policies that were breached by an employee. The company was also trying to decide whether or not to implement more invasive privacy programs within the workplace.

My team recommended terminating the employee, again keeping a no tolerance policy within the workplace with such important information. We also recommended to reinforce the privacy policy within the company through an internal memorandum, disband the more invasive privacy programs, and establish an employee hotline to catch future breaches in privacy.

UT AUSTIN NATIONAL WOMEN’S CASE COMPETITION 2021

Finalist
This case was a fictional situation written about Apple, the company that sponsored the event. They were going to launch a new product called the iBounce, a new concept pogo stick with technology embedded into it to enhance the experience. We were tasked with creating a marketing strategy, forecasting manufacturing and demand, and delivering a logistics strategy.

We proposed a marketing strategy surrounding fitness and staying active, and created personas, completed a competitive analysis, and designed social media campaign mockups for the product. We then mapped out the shipping and delivery logistics, keeping sustainability in mind.

CU BUSINESS ETHICS CASE COMPETITION 2021

First Place Winner
This case was based off of the current events surrounding the Black Lives Matter movement, and how a specific company should react. The first round of this competition introduced a satellite company that was trying to decide whether to endorse the Black Lives Matter movement, and if so, how to go about doing so.

My team recommended supporting the BLM movement. In addition, we proposed performing an internal audit to understand where the company could improve on its DEI processes, create new employee resource groups, and research potential solutions to address systemic inequalities present in current society.

Previous Next

The second part of the case included a past incident with the CEO participating in an offensive act, and our team had to consult on steps to continue.

We decided that the CEO shouldn’t resign because it was a minor mistake that could be corrected with a public apology and statement. We then dove deeper into the systemic inequality of the “Digital Divide” and proposed a program the company could initiate to bring lower income communities better internet and wifi access.

CU BCOR 1030: COMMUNICATIONS STRATEGY CASE COMPETITION 2020

Semifinalist
My first semester of college, I took a class called Communication Strategy. As a final project, we got to work on a case competition for an actual company. Our case focused on Illegal Pete’s, a local burrito restaurant, that was having trouble keeping up sales during the COVID-19 pandemic with social distancing mandates.

My team proposed two solutions to their problem. The first was a rewards program to encourage customers to order more often and increase returning customers through positive reinforcement. Our second solution was to launch an online cooking class with Illegal Pete’s chefs that could eventually move in-person once the pandemic was over.

CU DANIELS ETHICS CASE COMPETITION 2020

First Place Winner
This case presented an issue of an executive who was using his position to manipulate other employees to do extra work for him. Our team's job was to decide whether or not to terminate this executive, as well as develop a social media marketing plan to show the changes we planned to make within the company.

My team decided to take a no tolerance approach when deciding to terminate the executive, and redefine the values of the company partnered with an anonymous reporting system for all employees.
